Your Guide to the Halo Universe
Master Chief

Master Chief John-117 (usually referred to by his rank) was kidnapped at the age of 6 by the secretive UNSC agency ONI (Office of Naval Intelligence) after medical tests showed his neural activity was compatible with the experimental MJOLLNIR armor engineered as part of the SPARTAN-II program.

Along with 74 other children of the same age group, they were forced into a hard life of physical and mental training, as well as various bodily augmentations to both improve physical performance and heighten compatiblity with MJOLLNIR suits. While he usually operates on the field as part of Blue Team, most of the games showcase him as working without any fellow SPARTAN-II's.

Cortana

Cortana is a "smart" AI, and the first of her kind. Unlike other AI developed by the UNSC, Cortana has her own personality and acts akin to a human. Her original personality is a copy of her creator Dr. Halsey, who was also a key figure in the SPARTAN program.

Her technological capabilities are unparalleled, from infiltrating alien starships to storing the entirety of Alpha Halo's data. She can interface with virtually any computer, including the ones built into MJOLLNIR armor, and both communicate over radio and project herself as a small hologram.

The Arbiter

Thel'Vadam is a member of the Sangheli race of the Covenant (usually referred to by UNSC soldiers as "Elites"), and was tasked with the safekeeping of Alpha Halo. After the superweapon was destroyed at the end of Halo: Combat Evolved, the Covenant deemed him a heretic for failing to protect this vital artifact. While he was given a death sentence for heresy, he was in secret offered the title of "Arbiter", a line of historic figures sent out on suicidal missions that required stealthy, highly skilled operatives.

Upon his travels he met the "Oracle",in actuality the Monitor Guilty Spark-117 of Alpha Halo, who shared with him the truth of the Halo rings' destructive purpose. This revelation led to a schism in The Covenant with the Sangheli leaving to ally with humanity, and the Arbiter fighting alongside Master Chief.

Guilty Spark-343

Guilty Spark 343 is an AI created by the Forerunners to act as Monitor of Installation 04, also known as Alpha Halo. Its purpose is to ensure the maintenance of Alpha Halo, containment of Flood specimens, and activation of its firing sequence in the case of a Flood outbreak. After the destruction of Alpha Halo it is found by members of the Covenant, and is worshipped as an "Oracle", before it shares the true nature of Halo. Guilty Spark houses no ill intentions, and is seeking only to fulfill its preprogrammed instructions.

United Nations Space Command (UNSC)

The United Nations Space Command (UNSC) was formed as a singular military unit to handle extraterrestrial combat between Earth, Reach, and the various human colonies spread across the stars. However, when the Human-Covenant War began, the UNSC rapidly took command over all military assets, expending incredible amounts of resources in the hopes of preventing the extinction of mankind.

They also formed the covert Office of Naval Intelligence (ONI), which placed humanity's survival over individual rights or moderate loss of human life. This directly led to the creation of the SPARTAN program and Cortana, and by extension the defeat of the Covenant and eradication of the Flood.

The Covenant
The Covenant are a warmongering religion worshipping relics made by The Forerunners, not truly understanding the makers or their creations. The Covenant are comprised of multiple species of aliens, including the:
  • San'Shyuum ("Prophets")
  • Sangheili ("Elites")
  • Jiralhanae ("Brutes")
  • Lekgolo ("Hunters")
  • Unggoy ("Grunts")
  • Kig-Yar ("Jackals")
  • Yanme'e ("Drones")
  • Huragok("Engineers")
with each race having very distinct biology and combat abilities, as well as position in their caste system. During the Schism at the very end of the Human-Covenant War, the Sangheili abandoned the Covenant and took up arms alongside the humans to stop their ignorance and treachery.
The Flood
The Flood are a form of parasite that hijack living beings and transform them into abominations, seeking only to spread their control across other lifeforms. Flood parasites can "evolve" to higher states, the most powerful known being Gravemind, capable of controlling other Flood as part of a hivemind. Under its control the Flood are capable of using interstellar travel when available, potentially infecting all life. The Halo rings were initially created to destroy the Flood, but some specimens remained in containment in case future experimentation was needed.
The Forerunners
The Forerunners were a mysterious race that existed over 100,000 years ago, of a greater scale and with superior technology far beyond anything currently in the galaxy, including the creation of the Halo ringworlds. They activated the Halo array at the time of their demise to wipe out life on planets infected by The Flood (including themselves), only creating shield worlds on planets confirmed to be uninfected, including Earth.

Overview

The main Halo saga takes place across a war raging from the years 2525 and 2552. Humanity is stuggling to survive genocide by a religious empire of various alien species known as "The Covenant", who are seeking out artifacts from an ancient civilization known only as "The Forerunners".

Head among these religious icons are the Halo rings, which the Covenant believe will complete "The Great Journey" and bring salvation. However, the true design for these artificial ringworlds was that of mass annihilation, as the combined activation of the Halo rings would purge the entirety of the galaxy of life. The rings were designed to do so to wipe out a superparasite known as "The Flood" to prevent it from contaminating all life in the universe.

Background

The first entry in the franchise was the video game Halo: Combat Evolved, developed by Bungie Studios and published by Microsoft in 2001 as a killer app for their new console, the Xbox. Its combination of compelling storytelling, unique gameplay, and an unprecedented multiplayer experience made it a massive success, and it would later be compiled with other games in the series as part of the Master Chief Collection on Xbox One and PC.

This spawned a massive franchise consisting of games, books, comics, and animation. The latest game entry, Halo: Infinite is set to release on December 8, 2021, and will follow a post-war storyline where Master Chief must face a new alien group called the Banished across the ringworld Zeta Halo.
